What am I doing?

Addressing the housing crisis and densification agenda in mature urban neighbourhoods.

How am I doing this?

I have created a new business model and an architectural design tool-kit.

Why is this important to society?

To provide affordability, innovation and public acceptance within an established housing industry not normally so disposed.

This thesis is all about the big idea of land cost and its impacts.

The discovery that land cost is the biggest barrier to affordability changes everything.

What if the land is already there?

What if it is underutilized?

Can densification be respectful to neighbourhoods?

What ultimately evolves from this journey is a new housing / land acquisition model called BAAKFIL

BAAKFIL finds affordability by removing land cost from the developer proforma and passing the savings over to new purchasers

BAAKFIL is

Retaining

an original house and backfilling new housing on underused properties to yield smaller densification options that align with a changing demographic

Developing

a backyard strategy where underutilized land is made available for development, maintaining street and neighborhood character and urbanistic intentions

Creating

a business model where the landowner partners with a developer by contributing backyard space for development while retaining the original house and property

Utilizing

city alleys as skinny yield streets with a sidewalk, addressing, promoting more activity, safety, social supports and small scale, community mixed use.

Promoting

city building via neighborhood revitalization, giving agency to residents to self-organize

BAAKFIL

  • Original house retained
  • Neighbourhood character maintained
  • Landowner partners with developer
  • Landowner does not need to move
  • Land cost is removed from the proforma
  • Savings passed along to purchasers
  • The project is much more affordable

Infill

  • Original House demolished
  • Neighbourhood character disrupted
  • Landowner sells to developer
  • Landowner is displaced
  • Land cost is the bulk of the proforma
  • Land cost is passed along to purchasers
  • The project is unviable or unattainable
